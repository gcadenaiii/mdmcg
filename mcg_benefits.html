What is Magnetocardiography (MCG)?

Magnetocardiography (MCG) is a non-invasive medical imaging technique that detects the magnetic fields generated by the electrical activity of the heart. By providing highly sensitive and precise measurements, MCG offers a complementary tool to traditional cardiac diagnostics like ECG and echocardiography.

Key Benefits of Magnetocardiography

Non-Invasive

MCG requires no physical contact with the patient, ensuring comfort and reducing the risk of complications during testing.

High Sensitivity

MCG detects minute magnetic fields, providing a more detailed view of heart activity compared to traditional methods.

Early Detection

MCG can identify cardiac abnormalities at an earlier stage, allowing for timely intervention and treatment.

Radiation-Free

Unlike other imaging techniques, MCG does not expose the patient to radiation, making it safe for repeated use.

Enhanced Diagnostics

MCG offers complementary insights to other diagnostic tools like ECG, improving overall diagnostic accuracy.

Patient-Friendly

The procedure is quick, painless, and ideal for patients of all ages, including those with pacemakers.

Applications of MCG

Magnetocardiography is particularly useful for diagnosing ischemic heart disease, arrhythmias, and assessing patients with unexplained chest pain. It is also valuable in pre- and post-operative cardiac evaluations and research studies.
