MCG vs ECG: A Side-by-Side Comparison

Magnetocardiography (MCG)

  • Non-invasive and contactless procedure.
  • Highly sensitive to minute magnetic fields produced by cardiac activity.
  • Capable of early detection of cardiac abnormalities.
  • Radiation-free, ensuring safety for frequent testing.
  • Provides enhanced accuracy in identifying ischemia and arrhythmias.

Electrocardiography (ECG)

  • Requires physical electrodes to be placed on the body.
  • Limited sensitivity to subtle cardiac changes.
  • May miss early-stage abnormalities.
  • Cannot provide comprehensive 3D spatial data.
  • Less effective for detecting ischemic heart disease.

The Benefits of MCG Over ECG

While Electrocardiography has been a staple in cardiac diagnostics, Magnetocardiography offers a new level of precision and innovation. MCG excels at:

  • Detecting hidden cardiac issues that ECG may miss.
  • Improving diagnostic outcomes with higher sensitivity.
  • Allowing non-invasive and patient-friendly procedures.
  • Eliminating radiation exposure, ideal for frequent testing.
  • Enhancing early detection of ischemic heart disease and arrhythmias.
